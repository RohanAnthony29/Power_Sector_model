06 / Key Findings
01
Flat Load Eliminates Night Relief
Unlike residential load, data center demand is flat 24/7 — eliminating the overnight recovery window that historically allowed thermal plants to return online. This structurally raises the effective planning reserve requirement by 8–12 GW.
02
Battery Storage Is Insufficient Alone
4-hour battery storage reduces scarcity during transient peaks but cannot address multi-day stress events (heat domes, polar vortex). Long-duration storage or clean firm resources are needed to close the prolonged shortfall gap.
03
Demand Response Is High-Value First Step
Flexible DC demand response achieves the best reliability-per-dollar ratio at $90M/yr, reducing shortage hours significantly. Clean firm (SMR, geothermal) and transmission imports are most effective for extreme high-growth scenarios.
